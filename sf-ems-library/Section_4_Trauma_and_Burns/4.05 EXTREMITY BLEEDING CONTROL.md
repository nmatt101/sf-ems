BLS Treatment

•  Apply direct pressure to wound with a dressing.

o  Hemostatic gauze may be applied and wound may require packing.
o  Hold continuous pressure or apply a pressure dressing to wound.

•  Apply tourniquet 2-3 inches proximal to the bleeding site when:

o  Direct pressure does not control bleeding.
o  Amputation or near amputation of the limb.
o  Severe bleeding from a site which is not accessible (example: entrapment).
o  Severe bleeding from an impaled object.
o  When holding direct pressure is not practical.

If extremity deformed and pulseless, use gentle in line traction to restore anatomical
position.

•

•  Splint injured extremities if indicated. Cover open wounds with sterile dressing.

Re-check neurological function/circulation every 5 minutes.
