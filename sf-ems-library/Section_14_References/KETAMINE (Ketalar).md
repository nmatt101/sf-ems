Ketamine (Ketalar)

ACTION:  Dissociative anesthetic

•  N‐methyl‐Daspartate (NMDA) receptor agonist with a potent anesthetic effect.

INDICATION

Moderate to severe pain
where Ketorolac, Ibuprofen
or BLS measures may be
insufficient in controlling
pain.
Allergy to Fentanyl

ADULT/
PEDIATRIC (3 years and up)

• IV/IO 0.25mg/kg in 100ml of NS/D5W Slow infusion over 10

minutes

• IN 0.5 mg/kg
• Max dose for all routes is 30mg.
• Repeat dosing every 20 minutes, up to 2 doses.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
•
•
•
•

Pregnancy
Chest pain of cardiac origin
Hypersensitivity to Ketamine
Significant head trauma with altered mental status.

POTENTIAL SIDE EFFECTS:

•
•
•
•
•
•

Dizziness

Respiratory depression/Apnea

Nausea/vomiting

Hypertension
Impaired motor function
Laryngospasms

NOTES:

• Drowsiness
• Psychosis
• Amnesia
• Seizures

•  DO NOT co‐administer Fentanyl and ketamine at the same time. If administering Fentanyl

and Ketamine to the same patient, administer 15 minutes after completion of the first dose.

•  Cardiac monitoring with SpO2, EtCO2 and monitoring patient’s LOC is required
