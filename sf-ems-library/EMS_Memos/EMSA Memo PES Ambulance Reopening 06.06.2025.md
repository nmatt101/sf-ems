Subject: Psychiatric Emergency Services (PES) Ambulance Reopening (Direct Transport)

On June 10, 2025 at 0900, Psychiatric Emergency Services (PES) at Zuckerberg San Francisco General
Hospital (ZSFGH) is reopening to direct 9-1-1 ambulance transportation from the field. PES diversion
status is independent of ZSFGH Emergency Department (ED) ambulance diversion, trauma override or
APOT Alert status. PES status can be found in ReddiNet. PES should be utilized if open and patients
meet criteria under existing EMS Agency Policy 5000 (Destination Policy). Triage to Alternate Destination
(TAD) Accreditation is NOT required for PES.

Prior arrival to PES:

1)  Confirm patient meets criteria (Policy Reference No 5000 Section 6.11.6): Signs and symptoms
of psychiatric illness and are medically appropriate by meeting ALL of the following criteria:

a.  Age 18-65 years
b.  Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS) of 13 or greater
c.  Pulse rate between 55-120
d.  Systolic blood pressure between 90-190
e.  Diastolic blood pressure between 60-110
f.  Respiratory Rate between 12-24
g.  Temperature between 96.5 and 100.5 (or 35 to 38C)
h.  Oxygen saturation greater than 94%
i.  Blood glucose level between 60-250
j.  No active bleeding
k.  No bruising or hematoma above clavicles
l.  No active seizures
m.  No lacerations that have not been treated

2)  The following patients are not eligible for PES (Policy Reference No 500 Section 6.11.5.1 and

6.11.5.2):

a.  Patients who are severely agitated or combative and whose combativeness prevents an
assessment (vital signs or examination) and/or requires field sedation with midazolam.

Upon arrival to ZSFGH:

1)  Park in ED ambulance bay and enter via ED entrance
2)  San Francisco Sheriff Department will perform a security screening
3)  Notify ED triage that the patient is going directly to PES. Triage staff will call escort.
4)  Hospital staff will escort EMS personnel and utilize underground tunnel via STAFF elevator to
transport patient to PES while remaining on EMS gurney. Patient must be buckled during
transport. Do NOT use the trauma elevators.
