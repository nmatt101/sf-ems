2.16 SHOCK

BLS Treatment

ALS Treatment

If SBP < 90 or signs of poor perfusion, Normal Saline fluid bolus.

•  Establish IV/IO with Normal Saline TKO.
•
•  For suspected cardiogenic shock Epinephrine Infusion.  prepare 1 mcg/mL infusion.
Infuse at 1-3 drops/second IV/IO (6-18 mL/min) using 10 drops/mL macrodrip chamber.
Titrate to goal BP.

Comments

•  Compensated shock:  Anxiety, agitation, disorientation, tachycardia, normal B/P, capillary

refill normal to delayed, symptoms of allergic reaction, pallor, and/or diaphoresis.
•  Decompensated shock:  Decreased level of consciousness, tachycardia changing to

bradycardia, hypotension, delayed capillary refill, cyanosis, and/or unequal central and distal
pulses.

•  Follow Protocol 2.02 Allergic Reaction/Anaphylaxis if patient has suspected anaphylaxis.
