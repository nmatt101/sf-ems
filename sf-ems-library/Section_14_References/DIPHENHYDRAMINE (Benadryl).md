DIPHENHYDRAMINE (Benadryl)

ACTION: Antihistamine
•  Antagonizes effects of histamine in allergy.
•  Decreases itching, edema, bronchoconstriction, and vasodilation.
•  Antagonizes acetylcholine receptors, resulting in resolution of dystonia caused by antipsychotic

medications.

INDICATION
1. Anaphylaxis
2. Other allergic reaction
3. Extrapyramidal
symptoms, including
antipsychotic-induced
dystonia

ADULT
25 – 50 mg IVP/IO/IM
IM preferred if perfusing well.
IV preferred if anaphylaxis or
other severe allergic reaction.

PEDIATRIC
1 mg/kg IVP/IO/IM up to 25
mg
IM preferred if perfusing well.
IV preferred if anaphylaxis or
other severe allergic reaction.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
•  Relative contraindication in pregnant or lactating females

POTENTIAL SIDE EFFECTS:
•  Drowsiness and sedation
•  Hypotension
•  Palpitations
•  Potentiation of other CNS depressants

•  Tachycardia
•  Headache or blurred vision
•  Anticholinergic effects

NOTES:
•

In anaphylaxis, do not delay administration of epinephrine; administer diphenhydramine after
epinephrine.
